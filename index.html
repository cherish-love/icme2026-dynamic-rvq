Training-Free Dynamic RVQ Depth Allocation for Neural Audio Coding

Anonymous Authors

ICME 2026 Submission

Abstract

Residual Vector Quantization (RVQ) is fundamental to modern neural audio codecs, yet existing implementations apply a fixed depth across all frames, ignoring the inherent variability in audio complexity. This leads to over-quantization of simple frames and significant bitrate redundancy. We propose a training-free framework for dynamic RVQ depth allocation at inference time. Our method employs a lightweight complexity estimator analyzing latent features—energy, entropy, and dynamic range—to guide adaptive depth selection with negligible overhead. Evaluations on speech and music datasets demonstrate that our approach achieves perceptual quality matching fixed-depth baselines while reducing bitrate by 40% (2.69 vs. 4.50 kbps on speech). The framework requires no model retraining and serves as a plug-in solution for content-adaptive bitrate efficiency in existing RVQ-based codecs.
