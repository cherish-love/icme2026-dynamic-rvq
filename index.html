Training-Free Dynamic RVQ Depth Allocation for Neural Audio Coding

Anonymous Authors

Abstract

Residual Vector Quantization (RVQ) is a cornerstone of modern neural audio codecs, utilizing cascaded quantization layers to progressively refine latent representations for high-fidelity reconstruction. A prevalent limitation in current implementations is the use of a fixed RVQ depth for an entire audio sample, which overlooks inherent frame-wise variability in audio complexity, leading to significant bitrate redundancy. To overcome this inefficiency, we introduce a training-free framework for dynamic RVQ depth allocation operating solely at inference time. At its core is a lightweight complexity estimator analyzing latent features—energy, entropy, and dynamic range—to guide adaptive depth selection with negligible overhead. Extensive evaluations on speech and music datasets demonstrate that our method achieves perceptual quality equivalent to fixed-depth baselines while reducing bitrate by 40% (2.69 vs. 4.50 kbps). The framework requires no model retraining and serves as a plug-in solution for existing RVQ-based codecs.
